Textbook Practice

15 questions on calculating with negative integers, taken straight from the textbook (Exercise 1.1). Work each one out on paper, then reveal the worked answer to check.

15 questions · with worked answers
1
Fluency

11 − 5

Answer

11 − 5 = 6

2
Fluency

−2 − 3

Answer

Starting at −2 and going down 3 lands on −5.

3
Adding & subtracting negatives

12 − −15

Answer

Subtracting a negative is the same as adding: 12 + 15 = 27.

4
Adding & subtracting negatives

−26 + −18

Answer

Adding a negative is the same as subtracting: −26 − 18 = −44.

5
Difference

Work out the difference between −4 and −10.

Answer

Subtract the lower number from the higher: −4 − (−10) = −4 + 10 = 6.

6
Multiplying negatives

−2 × −4

Answer

Negative × negative = positive. 2 × 4 = 8.

7
Multiplying negatives

5 × (−9)

Answer

Positive × negative = negative. 5 × 9 = 45, so −45.

8
Multiplying negatives

−2 × −3 × −4

Answer

−2 × −3 = 6, then 6 × −4 = −24. Three negatives give a negative: −24.

9
Dividing negatives

132 ÷ −11

Answer

Positive ÷ negative = negative. 132 ÷ 11 = 12, so −12.

10
Dividing negatives

16 ÷ (−2) ÷ 2

Answer

Work left to right: 16 ÷ (−2) = −8, then −8 ÷ 2 = −4.

11
Expanding brackets

6 × (−2 − 1)

Answer

Brackets first: −2 − 1 = −3, then 6 × −3 = −18.

12
Expanding brackets

−3(−4 − 1)

Answer

Brackets first: −4 − 1 = −5, then −3 × −5 = 15.

13
Substituting into a formula

Work out m = 2n − 1 when n = −7.

Answer

m = 2 × (−7) − 1 = −14 − 1 = −15.

14
Squaring negatives

(−3)2

Answer

(−3)2 = −3 × −3 = 9.

15
Square roots

Write the positive and negative square roots of 121.

Answer

11 × 11 = 121 and −11 × −11 = 121, so the roots are +11 and −11.
