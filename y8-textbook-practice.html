Year 8 · Number

Textbook Practice

15 multi-step questions on calculating with negative integers, taken straight from the textbook (Exercise 1.1) — reasoning, worded problems, statistics and substituting into formulae. Work each one out on paper, then reveal the worked answer to check.

15 questions · with worked answers
1
STEM · worded

When hydrogen gas is cooled, it becomes a liquid at −253 °C and freezes solid at −259 °C. Hydrogen gas at −160 °C is cooled by 100 °C. After cooling, is it a gas, liquid or solid?

Answer

New temperature = −160 − 100 = −260 °C. That is below the freezing point of −259 °C, so it is solid.

2
STEM · worded

Hydrogen becomes a liquid at −253 °C. In a science lab, hydrogen has a temperature of 20 °C. By how many degrees do you need to cool it so that it becomes a liquid?

Answer

Find the difference: 20 − (−253) = 20 + 253 = 273 °C.

3
Reasoning · sign rules

Copy and complete the rules for multiplying:
positive × positive = positive  ·  positive × negative = …
negative × positive = …  ·  negative × negative = …

Answer

positive × negative = negative, negative × positive = negative, negative × negative = positive. Same signs give a positive answer; different signs give a negative answer.

4
Brackets & order of operations

3 × (−1 + 4) − 13

Answer

Brackets first: −1 + 4 = 3. Then 3 × 3 = 9, and 9 − 13 = −4.

5
Brackets & order of operations

−4(−3 + 5) − 2

Answer

Brackets first: −3 + 5 = 2. Then −4 × 2 = −8, and −8 − 2 = −10.

6
Statistics · solar panel data

The power (W) sent to the grid each 10 minutes was: −130, 220, −1395, 640, −1565, −290. Work out the median.

Answer

In order: −1565, −1395, −290, −130, 220, 640. With six values the median is the mean of the middle two: (−290 + −130) ÷ 2 = −210.

7
Statistics · solar panel data

Using the same values (−130, 220, −1395, 640, −1565, −290), work out the range.

Answer

Range = highest − lowest = 640 − (−1565) = 640 + 1565 = 2205.

8
Statistics · solar panel data

Using the same values (−130, 220, −1395, 640, −1565, −290), work out the mean.

Answer

Sum = −130 + 220 − 1395 + 640 − 1565 − 290 = −2520. Mean = −2520 ÷ 6 = −420.

9
Substituting into a formula

Substitute to find m = 2n − 1 when n = −7.

Answer

m = 2 × (−7) − 1 = −14 − 1 = −15.

10
Substituting into a formula

Substitute to find v = u + at when u = −8, a = −10 and t = 6.

Answer

v = −8 + (−10 × 6) = −8 + (−60) = −68.

11
Substituting into a formula

Substitute to find A = 3a − 4b when a = −2 and b = −5.

Answer

A = 3 × (−2) − 4 × (−5) = −6 + 20 = 14.

12
Substituting into a formula

Substitute to find T = k(e − f) when k = −3, e = 4 and f = −2.

Answer

Brackets first: e − f = 4 − (−2) = 6. Then T = −3 × 6 = −18.

13
Substituting into a formula

Substitute to find L = a − (2b + c) when a = −10, b = −8 and c = 4.

Answer

Inside the bracket: 2 × (−8) + 4 = −16 + 4 = −12. Then L = −10 − (−12) = −10 + 12 = 2.

14
Reasoning · squaring negatives

Is it possible to square a negative number and get an answer that is also negative? Explain.

Answer

No. Squaring means multiplying a number by itself, so the two factors always have the same sign. A negative times a negative is positive (e.g. (−6)2 = −6 × −6 = 36), so the answer is never negative.

15
Square roots

Write the positive and negative square roots of 144.

Answer

12 × 12 = 144 and −12 × −12 = 144, so the roots are +12 and −12.
